## 4. Municipal Architecture

### 4.1 City Hall (The Comptroller)
**Role:** Central Command & Governance.
The Comptroller is the brain of the city. It issues permits, manages the Treasury, and audits system processes. It serves as the authoritative source for network topology and service allocation, ensuring no collisions occur within the municipal grid.

### 4.2 Jimenez Military Base (Defense)
**Role:** Network Security & Threat Neutralization.
The Military Base monitors the city's perimeter. It enforces security protocols based on active Governance Directives and maintains a "Full Spectrum" watch over non-standard access points to detect anomalies before they breach the walls.

### 4.3 Jimenez Int'l Airport (Logistics)
**Role:** Transaction Processing & Packet Routing.
The Airport visualizes the Mempool as "Incoming Flights." Every pending transaction is treated as a plane seeking permission to land. The Airport conducts rigorous "Customs & Border Control" checks to validate signatures and limits before allowing entry into the city's ledger.

## 5. Economic Model: Sovereign Valuation
The economy is backed by **Sovereign Infrastructure**, not just speculation.
*   **Sovereign Assets:** The primary store of value. These are "minted" via a proprietary process that simulates the synthesis of value from raw energy.
*   **Municipal Coin:** The medium of exchange for municipal services.
*   **Valuation Logic:** The system recognizes the "Book Value" of its own infrastructure, calculating valuation based on the aggregate of held assets and energy potential rather than volatile market sentiment.

## 6. Security & Privacy
*   **Dual-Layer Physical Vaults:** Critical operations require physical hardware keys.
*   **Local-Only Signing:** Private keys never leave the secure memory of the signing environment.
*   **Stealth Mode:** Deployment infrastructure utilizes system-level masking to remain invisible to the host operating system.
*   **Sovereign Sync:** Offline Vaults maintain a "One-Way Uplink" capability. They can read Governance Directives from the Municipal Grid to update security protocols without ever exposing private keys to the internet.
*   **Protocol Omega:** A terminal security protocol for high-risk assets. If specific threat conditions are met, the Vault initiates a sector-level overwrite sequence, rendering the device physically irrecoverable to protect the sovereign state.
